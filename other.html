SUPER MARIO PCG

As a project to train my artificial intelligence skills, I rebuilt Super Mario Bros from scratch with a procedural content generation system to make random levels out of predesigned chunks represented by two-dimensional character arrays. Each chunk also notes its starting and ending elevation in order to select for smoother, more natural level layouts.

This project was completed in four days and co-developed by Daniel Onyema.

CLOCK KNIGHT

As a re-styled advancement on the Mario PCG project for an international game jam, Clock Knight is a procedurally-generated platformer. The knight is on a strict time limit of ten seconds to reach the goal, which he replenishes by defeating enemies and collecting coins.

This project was completed in one week and co-developed by Daniel Onyema.

GUN SHRIMP

Perlenspiel is a basic game programming engine made in Javascript by Brian Moriarty. With just a grid of beads for a screen and a line of status text, it's designed to be constrictive in a way that encourages creativity. It's used in WPI's Digital Game Design course (IMGD 2900). For the final project, I was required to construct a Metaphor Game, in which the game mechanics are tied to some sort of visual indicator. For this project, I created Gun Shrimp, a simple arcade-style game where you play as a shrimp trying to shoot the enemy crab as much as possible without it shooting you back. The game gets increasingly difficult as you score points, as the crab becomes faster and more aggressive.
